# Student guide

1. Enter a supported formula such as H2O, NH3, XeF4, NO3-, or CO3^2-, or choose an example.
2. Check total valence electrons and formal charges in the Lewis card. The formal-charge sum must equal the ion charge.
3. Count bonding domains and lone-pair domains around the central atom. A double or triple bond still counts as one domain.
4. Read AXnEm: X is the number of bonded atoms and E is the number of lone-pair domains.
5. Separate electron-domain geometry from molecular geometry; lone pairs affect the first but are not visible atoms in the second.
6. Rotate/zoom the 3D model and compare it with your prediction. The fallback model is idealized, not an experimental structure.
7. Use the explanation after making your own prediction. AI cannot change the checked chemistry facts.

Supported syntax is flat. Parentheses, hydrates, coefficients, isotopes, transition-metal complexes, and arbitrary organic structures are outside this version.

Survey and feedback are anonymous. Do not enter your name, student number, email, or other identifying details. Participation is voluntary.
